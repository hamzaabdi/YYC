Key Observations

Steady Cost Escalation

Both provinces show persistent transportation cost increases since 2002, with Alberta consistently higher. This reflects fuel price sensitivity, longer freight distances, and car-dependent infrastructure in Alberta's sprawling geography.

Alberta's Higher Volatility

Alberta's CPI shows sharper swings during commodity price shocks (2008, 2014, 2022). The province's oil-dependent economy amplifies fuel cost pass-through to trucking and freight sectors, creating margin pressure for logistics operators.

BC's Relative Stability

British Columbia's more urban, transit-oriented infrastructure provides some insulation from fuel price spikes. Shorter intra-provincial hauls and multimodal transport options (ferries, rail) help moderate cost volatility compared to Alberta.

Shock Events (2008, 2022)

Price surges align with global oil spikes and supply chain disruptions. The 2022 spike reflects post-pandemic logistics bottlenecks, driver shortages, and elevated diesel costs. These events disproportionately impact Calgary's freight-intensive economy.

What This Means for Calgary

Higher Input Costs: Elevated Alberta transport CPI increases logistics expenses for Calgary businesses. Freight, warehousing, and last-mile delivery costs rise, compressing profit margins and potentially driving consumer price inflation.

Supply Chain Pressure: Calgary's landlocked position makes it highly dependent on road and rail freight. Rising transport costs reduce competitiveness versus coastal competitors with cheaper port access, affecting export-oriented industries.

Labor Market Impact: Higher fuel and operating costs strain trucking companies, contributing to driver shortages and wage pressure. Calgary logistics firms face recruitment challenges and must invest in retention strategies.

Efficiency Opportunities: Rising costs incentivize investment in route optimization, warehouse automation, and electric/hybrid fleets. Calgary businesses adopting logistics technology can offset inflation impacts and gain competitive advantages.
