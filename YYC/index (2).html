What This Means for Calgary

Energy Sector Impact

Rising WTI prices strengthen Calgary's energy sector outlook. Higher crude values drive increased capital investment, job creation, and corporate earnings for the city's dominant industry cluster.

Currency Pressure

A weaker Canadian dollar makes imports more expensive but boosts export competitiveness for Alberta's agricultural products and manufactured goods destined for U.S. markets.

Logistics Costs Rising

Elevated transport CPI signals increased freight and fuel costs. Calgary businesses face higher input costs, affecting profit margins and pricing strategies across supply chains.

Agriculture Surge

Record wheat export volumes demonstrate strong global demand for Alberta grain. This drives revenue for agricultural producers and supports Calgary's agribusiness ecosystem.
